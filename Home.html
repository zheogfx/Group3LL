This Page gives you a general idea about typhoon odette and how it affected the phillipines.

What is the basic information of the typhoon? (Typhoon rai)

Typhoon Rai, known locally as Typhoon Odette, struck the Philippines in December 2021. It was a powerful
Category 5-equivalent super typhoon and sustained winds of around 195 km/h (120 mph) and gusts up to
km/h (150 mph) This typhoon brought heavy rainfall, causing widespread flooding and landslides in many areas
including Surigao del Norte, Siargao Island, and Dinagat Islands, before moving across the Visayas and parts
of Luzon.

What is the basic information of the region that has been greatly affected by the typhoon?

The region greatly affected by Typhoon Rai in the Philippines includes parts of Caraga, Eastern Visayas, Central
Visayas, and Northern Mindanao. This is because, geographically, these areas are located in the eastern and central
parts of the country, facing the Pacific Ocean, making them vulnerable to typhoons. That's why when the typhoon
comes, most of their diverse industries are also affected, such as agriculture, fishing and manufacturing.

How was the community affected by the typhoon?

This typhoon destroyed Houses, Properties and livelihoods due to the strong winds and floods. It was also reported
that a massive amount of 36 million houses were damaged or destroyed causing thousands of people becoming homeless.
This typhoon also caused power outages, Improper function of water, Damages bridges, Schools, and roads.

How Did the Community Prepare and Recover?

A humanitarian group provided 4 million pesos of digital cash transfers, which helped over 2,000 families to prepare and buy necessary materials before typhoon Odette hits. These materials may include food, medicine, medical kits, batteries and other essentials. But after the typhoon the LGU also provided 4.85 billion pesos to help assist the people affected by typhoon Odette.
This amount was then distributed to each region then to the affected families. Each household would get a maximum amount
of 5 thousand pesos granting each person 1,000 pesos.

How vulnerable are they to typhoons?

As said previously, This is because areas such as Visayas and Mindanao Islands are located in the eastern
and central parts of the country making them vulnerable to typhoons. This means that these areas may experience
storm surges, violent winds, and torrential rains.

Were there any damages? How severe they were?

Super Typhoon Rai, also referred to as Odette locally, caused severe damage, taking the lives of at least 409 individuals while damaging or completely destroying almost 1.7 million houses. The tropical storm increased the difficulties experienced by the impacted people by seriously damaging infrastructure, including 220 health facilities and rendering 36% of harbors unusable.

Was the typhoon accompanied by secondary disasters? How did they affect the community?

Typhoon Odette swept all over through 11 of the countries 17 regions, landslide and flooding is the cost of the typhoon,
leaving behind a trail of flattened homes, damaged farms, collapsed bridges, and other food sources, severely affecting peoples lives and
livelihoods.
